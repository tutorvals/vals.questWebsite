# Roleplay games

I'm keen to organise tabletop RPG sessions and to GM, especially if someone else would be willing to GM another time.

The classic introductory RPG offer is a one-shot with no rules or light rules. You can do short sessions of 2-3 hours but 3-4 hours is more typical.

I have a preference for groups of 3-5 players (limit 6). For 7+ people, something like a [murder party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murder_mystery_game) works better than classic tabletop RPG.

I enjoy introducing new players to RPGs, but I'd also be curious to run something more complex if people want to level up (it trains rationality, apparently — it's a bit like nailing the scenarios in the board game Paleo on the first try).

## Systems I can GM

### Call of Cthulhu (intro) — mystery, horror, investigation

An RPG that starts a bit fantastical and quickly goes into cosmic horror. (Well, if you end up mad, maybe you were mad all along?)

> In Call of Cthulhu, you take on the role of everyday people who become investigators of the unknown — whether they are prepared or not. The mysterious places, people, and situations you encounter are often not what they seem — you and your friends are the only thing standing in the way of diabolical cults and cosmic monsters from beyond space!

So investigate, investigate — you might survive.

### Shadow Musketeers (intro) — adventure, panache, bravery, investigation

In 1654, an alien spaceship crashes in the woods of Fontainebleau, carrying dangerous convicts from across the galaxy. Regent Mazarin seizes the affair, makes a pact with the alien ship commander, and creates an elite unit: the Shadow Musketeers. You play swashbucklers blending bravery and panache to track down the most incongruous aliens — like Men in Black, but in the age of musketeers.

### Ars Magica (intro) — adventure, strategy/tactics

Set in mythical Arthurian medieval Europe (where Arthurian legends and similar myths are more or less true), centered around a Covenant — a kind of school of magic with labs and advanced studies. Characters are often a mix of mages, companions, and mundane folk, seeking to grow their covenant by solving local problems or seeking precious resources.

### Orc Invasion (intro)

We play as ourselves, in the 21st century, but strange things start happening... (It could be an orc invasion, literally orcs opening a portal from another world, or it could be something fantastical or Narnia-like or...)

### Shadowrun (medium/complex)

> Shadowrun is a science fantasy tabletop role-playing game set in an alternate future in which cybernetics, magic and fantasy creatures co-exist. It combines genres of cyberpunk, urban fantasy, and crime, with occasional elements of conspiracy, horror, and detective fiction.

## Systems I'd like to try GMing

### Paranoia — science fiction, survival, dark humour

You are aboard a spaceship that has left Earth. The Computer takes care of you. It gives you your missions and responsibilities on board. Be sure to report traitor commie-mutants at every opportunity.

### Star Wars

### Science fiction based on one of my dreams

You wake up in an abandoned train, or a hangar, or something. You have some memories of who you are but not much more than that. The goal is to figure out where you are, who you are, and what's going on.
